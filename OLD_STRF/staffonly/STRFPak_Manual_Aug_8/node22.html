STRFs with large TimeLags or large-dimensional stimuli will have so many free parameters that a form of regularization is desired. STRFPAKs regularization works by projecting the STRF onto a subspace spanned by the strongest few eigenvectors of the stimulus autocorrelation. Tolerance value controls how many eigenvectors are included in this subspace. All eigenvectors with a corresponding eigenvalue less than a given tolerance value are set to zero in the estimated STRF. Since it is difficult to guess what the tolerance value should be for any cell a priori, and since estimating and validating a STRF at several tolerance values is computationally easy, a list of trial tolerance values are typically used.
