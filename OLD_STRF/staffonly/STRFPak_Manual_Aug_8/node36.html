STRFPAK uses an unbiased measure of how well the prediction would match the true PSTH given infinite data size. To do this, STRFPAK compares how well one spike train is expected to fit the extrapolated infinite data PSTH versus how well one spike train is expected to fit the prediction. The first of these is the ``r'' displayed in the cc window, the second is the ``r predicted''. The ratio of ``r predicted'' to ``r'' is called the cc ratio. A perfectly linear neuron characterized perfectly by a STRF should have a cc ratio of 1. The cc ratio will be a function of the smoothing window size. Depending on the researchers perspective, it may be most appropriate to look at the cc ratio of the cells at the smoothing window giving the maximum cc ratio, or it may be better to impose a fixed smoothing window length so that comparisons across many cells are more fair.
