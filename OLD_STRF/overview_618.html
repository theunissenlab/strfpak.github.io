What is an STRF?

There is increasing evidence that sensory systems have been optimized through evolution to process behaviorally relevant natural stimuli. The spatio-temporal receptive field (STRF) provides a quantitative description of the (non)linear transformation between a time varying spatial stimulus and the neural response. STRFs were first applied to neurons in the auditory system, but are now commonly used in other sensory modalities (Theunissen et al, 2001).

Why use STRFPAK?

Although the theoretical basis for STRF estimation has been known for some time, general application of these algorithms has been hampered by the lack of convenient and general software. The goal of this project is to develop such software and to make it available to the wider community of sensory neuroscientists. Although STRFPAK is based on established methods, it incorporates two significant extensions. First STRFPAK can be used to characterize neurons from their respones to arbitrary, complex stimuli, including natural signals (e.g., bird songs, natural scenes). Second, STRFPAK incorporates several quantitative methods for estimating nonlinear STRFs. To facilitate use of STRFPAK, tutorial examples and help documents are also provided.
